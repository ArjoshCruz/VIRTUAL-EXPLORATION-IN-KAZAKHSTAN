About

Kazakhstan is the largest landlocked country in the world and the ninth-largest overall, covering 2.7 million square kilometers. Located in Central Asia with a small portion in Eastern Europe, it shares borders with Russia, China, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan. Kazakhstan boasts a diverse landscape, featuring vast steppes, deserts, mountains, and lakes. The Caspian Sea lies to its west, providing a unique contrast to its otherwise landlocked status. The country is rich in natural resources, including oil, gas, and minerals, which play a key role in its economy.

Language

The official language of Kazakhstan is Kazakh, which is a Turkic language written in the Cyrillic script. Russian is also widely spoken and serves as a co-official language in government, business, and daily life, especially in urban areas. Many Kazakh citizens are bilingual in Kazakh and Russian.

Geography & Climate

Kazakhstan has a diverse landscape of steppes, deserts, and mountains. The country experiences a continental climate, with extreme temperature differences between summer and winter.

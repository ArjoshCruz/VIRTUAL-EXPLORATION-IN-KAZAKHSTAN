Places & Activities

Kazakhstan offers a wide range of activities for visitors to enjoy, from exploring its natural wonders to experiencing its rich culture and history. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this diverse country.

Outdoor Activities

1. Explore Charyn Canyon

I think Charyn Canyon is one of the best attractions in Kazakhstan because it reminds me of the Grand Canyon, but with its own unique charm. Located about 200 km east of Almaty, this stunning natural wonder stretches for around 154 km. I think that the towering rock formation that is a good place to see because it is all natural and was made over millions of years.

  • Highlights: The famous "Valley of Castles," where the rock formations resemble ancient fortresses.
  • Activities: Hiking, photography, camping, and rafting on the Charyn River.
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) for mild weather.

2. Hike in the Altai Mountains

The Altai Mountains, located in the easternmost part of Kazakhstan, are a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. They are known for lush forests, glacial lakes, and diverse wildlife, including snow leopards and bears. I love nature and especially landscapes, so I think this is a great place to visit for people who loves hiking and nature seeing.

  • Highlights: The stunning Belukha Mountain (4,506 m), the highest peak in the Altai range.
  • Activities: Trekking, wildlife spotting, horseback riding, and camping.
  • Best Time to Visit: June to September for the best hiking conditions.

3. Visit Big Almaty Lake

Big Almaty Lake is a breathtaking alpine reservoir located about 15 km south of Almaty, at an altitude of 2,511 meters. I think that this is a great place to visit because of its stunning turquoise color, which changes depending on the season.

  • Highlights: Surrounded by the Tien Shan mountains, it offers postcard-worthy views.
  • Activities: Sightseeing, photography, hiking, and picnicking (swimming is prohibited).
  • Best Time to Visit: Late spring to early autumn (May-September) for the best scenery.

4. Skiing in Shymbulak

Shymbulak (also called Chimbulak) is Kazakhstan’s top ski resort, located just 25 km from Almaty in the Tien Shan mountains. The resort is famous for its well-maintained slopes and modern facilities.

  • Highlights: Ski runs up to 3,200 meters high, offering breathtaking mountain views.
  • Activities: Skiing, snowboarding, cable car rides, and dining at high-altitude cafes.
  • Best Time to Visit: December to March for skiing, but the resort is also great for summer hiking.

5. See the Almaty Singing Dunes

The Singing Dunes are a unique natural phenomenon located in Altyn-Emel National Park, about 300 km from Almaty. The 150-meter-high dunes produce a humming or singing sound when the wind moves the sand. This place is a unique destination to visit because of the combination of breathtaking views, the mysterious singing sound, and the thrill of climbing the dunes makes it an unforgettable experience.

  • Highlights: The dunes, nearby Aktau (White Mountains), and Katutau volcanic mountains.
  • Activities: Sandboarding, hiking, and listening to the natural "singing" sound.
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) for pleasant weather.

Historical Activities

1. Visit the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi

Located in Turkistan, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of Kazakhstan’s most significant historical and architectural landmarks. Built in the 14th century by order of Tamerlane (Timur), the mausoleum was meant to honor the great Sufi mystic Khoja Ahmed Yasawi, who played a crucial role in spreading Islam in Central Asia.

  • Highlights: The largest brick dome in Central Asia, stunning blue-tiled exterior, and well-preserved ancient calligraphy.
  • Activities: Explore the intricate Islamic architecture, visit the underground prayer room, and learn about Yasawi’s spiritual legacy.
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and autumn (March-May, September-November) for comfortable weather.

2. See the Bayterek Tower

Located in Astana (Nur-Sultan), Bayterek Tower is one of Kazakhstan’s most recognizable landmarks. The tower stands 97 meters tall, symbolizing the year 1997, when Astana became the capital. Designed to resemble a golden egg held in a tree, the tower is inspired by a Kazakh legend about the mythical bird Samruk.

  • Highlights: A 360-degree panoramic view of Astana from the observation deck.
  • Activities: Visit the top of the tower, see the golden handprint of former President Nursultan Nazarbayev, and enjoy the futuristic cityscape.
  • Best Time to Visit: Year-round, but evenings offer the best city views.

Unique & Fun Activities

1. Watch a traditional Kokpar (Buzkashi) match

Kokpar is Kazakhstan’s national horseback game, similar to Buzkashi in neighboring Central Asian countries. Often called the "sport of the steppes," it involves two teams of skilled horse riders competing to grab a goat carcass and score points by carrying it into a goal.

  • Highlights: Fast-paced, intense competition showcasing horsemanship and teamwork.
  • Where to Watch: Major cities like Almaty, Astana, and Shymkent often host Kokpar tournaments, especially during Nauryz (Persian New Year in March) and other national celebrations.
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and autumn, when large Kokpar tournaments take place.

2. Try Kumis (fermented mare’s milk)

Kumis is a traditional Kazakh drink made from fermented mare’s milk. It has been a staple of nomadic diets for centuries, believed to have health benefits due to its probiotics and vitamins. The taste is slightly sour and tangy, with mild alcohol content due to the fermentation process.

  • Where to Try: Available in yurts, local markets, and traditional restaurants across Kazakhstan. Some farms offer tours where visitors can see the milking process.
  • Health Benefits: Rich in vitamins A, C, and E, Kumis is said to aid digestion and boost immunity.
  • Best Time to Try: Spring and summer, when mares produce the most milk.

3. Take a Soviet-style metro ride in Almaty

Almaty’s Soviet-era metro system is one of the most beautiful and efficient in Central Asia. Opened in 2011, it features stunning underground stations decorated with Kazakh art, mosaics, and marble columns.

  • Highlights: The Abay Station, inspired by Kazakh poet Abay Kunanbayev, and the Baikonur Station, named after the famous space launch site.
  • Why Ride it?: It’s clean, affordable, and a piece of Soviet-era nostalgia mixed with modern design.
  • Best Time to Visit: Any time, but try riding during non-peak hours to appreciate the station designs.
